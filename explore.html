Ancient Places Of India


Brihadeeswarar Temple

The Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its architectural brilliance and cultural significance. Built by the Chola dynasty in the 11th century, it stands as a testament to Dravidian architecture.

Taj Mahal


The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Agra, India, is an architectural masterpiece and a symbol of eternal love. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, its stunning white marble structure.

Chandragiri Fort

Chandragiri Fort, located in Andhra Pradesh, India, is a historic site dating back to the 11th century. Built by the Yadava dynasty and later fortified by the Vijayanagara Empire, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

The Sun Temple

The Sun Temple, located in Konark, Odisha, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its intricate architecture and religious significance. Built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty.

The Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the tallest brick minarets in the world. Built in the 12th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, it stands as a remarkable example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

The Shore Temple

The Shore Temple, situated in Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site dating back to the 8th century. Constructed during the reign of the Pallava dynasty, it stands as a testament to Dravidian architecture.

Meenatchi Temple

The Meenakshi Amman Temple, located in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India, is a historic Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (Parvati) and her consort Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva). Known for its towering gopurams .

Ancient Stepwells

In Gujarat, India, ancient stepwells called "vavs" dot the landscape, showcasing remarkable architectural and engineering feats. Rani ki Vav in Patan, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the most elaborate examples.

Auroville

Auroville, located in Tamil Nadu, India, is an experimental township founded in 1968 with the vision of human unity and sustainable living. Designed by architect Roger Anger, its centerpiece is the Matrimandir.
